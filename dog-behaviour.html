Understanding Dog Behaviour

Dogs communicate mainly through body language. Reading signs early can prevent stress, bites, and fear.

Body Language Signals

  • Loose body + soft eyes: usually relaxed.
  • Tail tucked, ears back: fear or uncertainty.
  • Frozen body, hard stare: high risk warning.

Signs of Happiness

  • Wiggly movement and relaxed mouth.
  • Play bow (front low, back high).
  • Willingness to approach and engage.

Fear or Anxiety Signs

  • Yawning, lip licking, turning away.
  • Hiding, trembling, pacing.
  • Refusing food in stressful settings.

⚠ Aggression Warning Signs

  • Growling, snarling, snapping.
  • Raised hackles and rigid posture.
  • Guarding food, toys, or space.

Stop interaction immediately and increase distance.

Stress Behaviour

Repeated barking, chewing, digging, and over-grooming can indicate stress, boredom, or poor routine.

Support stress reduction with predictable feeding, exercise, and quiet rest areas.

Why Dogs Bark, Growl, or Hide

Barking can mean excitement, fear, alerting, or frustration. Growling is often a warning, not "bad behavior." Hiding can mean pain, fear, or overwhelm.

Safe Ways to Approach and Build Trust

  1. Approach slowly from the side, not straight on.
  2. Offer space; let the dog come first.
  3. Avoid direct staring and sudden reaching.
  4. Use a calm voice and reward calm behaviour.
  5. Repeat short, positive interactions to build trust.
Observe first, act gently, and respect boundaries.
