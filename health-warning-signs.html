Health & Warning Signs

Early detection saves lives. If you notice sudden changes, seek professional advice quickly.

🩺 Signs of Illness

  • Loss of appetite or thirst changes
  • Vomiting, diarrhoea, or constipation
  • Coughing, sneezing, discharge

🔄 Behavioural Changes

  • Unusual hiding or clinginess
  • Sudden aggression or confusion
  • Low energy and no interest in play

🩹 Injury Indicators

  • Limping or reluctance to move
  • Swelling, bleeding, open wounds
  • Frequent licking of one area

🚨 Emergency Warning Signs

  • Difficulty breathing
  • Collapse or seizures
  • Pale gums or heavy bleeding
  • Suspected poisoning

Go to emergency veterinary care immediately.

Pain Symptoms

Whining, panting at rest, stiff movement, avoiding touch, and sudden behaviour changes may signal pain.

When to Seek Help

If symptoms last over 24 hours, worsen quickly, or include fever, dehydration, or bleeding, contact a vet right away.

Monitoring & Prevention Tips

  • Track appetite, water intake, stool quality, and activity.
  • Schedule regular wellness checks.
  • Keep vaccines and parasite prevention up to date.
  • Maintain clean living spaces and balanced nutrition.
Trust your instincts—early action is safer than waiting.
