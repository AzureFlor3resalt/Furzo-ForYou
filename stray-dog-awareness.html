Stray Dog Awareness & Safety

Stray dogs may react differently due to hunger, fear, pain, or past trauma. Safety and humane treatment should always go together.

Why Strays Behave Differently

Limited food, no stable shelter, disease risk, and past negative human contact can make stray dogs cautious or defensive.

When Strays Feel Threatened

Approaching quickly, cornering, shouting, or competing near food can trigger defensive behaviour.

Safe Behaviour Around Strays

  • Stay calm and move slowly.
  • Keep distance; do not crowd.
  • Avoid eye contact and sudden gestures.

What NOT to Do

  • Do not run toward or away in panic.
  • Do not throw objects or hit the dog.
  • Do not try to grab puppies.

Signs a Stray May Attack

  • Rigid body, direct stare, growling
  • Teeth showing, repeated lunging
  • Raised hackles and circling

If a Stray Approaches

  1. Freeze, keep arms close.
  2. Avoid screaming or waving hands.
  3. Back away slowly toward safety.
  4. Use an object (bag, bike) as a barrier if needed.

When to Contact Local Authorities

Report injured, aggressive, or sick stray dogs to local animal welfare or municipal control teams. Encourage rescue, vaccination, and sterilisation—not cruelty.

Community responsibility: Support humane programs, public awareness, and safe waste management to reduce conflict.

Safety first, kindness always.
