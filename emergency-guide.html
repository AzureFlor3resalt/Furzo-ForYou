Emergency Guide: Fast Action Steps

This page is designed for quick reading in urgent situations.

Emergency Symptoms Checklist

  • Breathing difficulty or choking
  • Seizures or collapse
  • Severe bleeding or trauma
  • Suspected poisoning
  • Heatstroke signs: excessive panting, weakness, vomiting

Immediate Response

  1. Stay calm and secure the area.
  2. Move dog away from danger if safe.
  3. Call vet/emergency clinic immediately.
  4. Follow phone guidance while transporting.

Basic First Aid Guidance

  • Apply clean pressure to bleeding wounds.
  • Keep dog warm and still after injury.
  • Do not give human medicine unless instructed by a vet.

Urgent Help Required If...

Symptoms are severe, sudden, worsening, or involve breathing, consciousness, poisoning, or uncontrolled pain.

Quick Decision Flow Chart

1. Is the dog breathing normally? No → Call emergency vet now.
2. Is there heavy bleeding, seizure, collapse, or poisoning? Yes → Immediate transport.
3. Mild symptoms only? Monitor closely and contact vet for same-day advice.
4. If unsure at any step, treat it as urgent and seek professional help.
Keep local emergency vet contacts saved in your phone.
