Vaccinations Protect Dogs, Families, and Communities

Vaccination is one of the most important acts of responsible ownership. It prevents serious disease and reduces public health risk.

Purpose of Dog Vaccinations

Vaccines train the immune system to fight dangerous infections before dogs become seriously ill.

How Vaccines Protect Dogs

Vaccinated dogs are far less likely to develop severe symptoms, complications, or death from preventable diseases.

How Vaccines Protect Humans

Rabies and other diseases can spread between animals and people. Vaccination reduces community exposure.

Common Preventable Diseases

  • Rabies
  • Canine parvovirus
  • Canine distemper
  • Adenovirus/hepatitis
  • Leptospirosis (region dependent)

Vaccination Schedule

Puppies usually begin vaccines around 6–8 weeks, with repeat doses every few weeks until immune protection is complete.

Regular Boosters

Boosters maintain protection over time. Follow your veterinarian's local schedule recommendations.

Risks of Not Vaccinating

Unvaccinated dogs face preventable suffering, higher treatment costs, and increased risk to other animals and people.

Community Health & Responsible Ownership

Keeping your dog vaccinated is an ethical commitment. It protects neighbours, children, stray populations, and vulnerable pets.

Vaccinate on time, keep records, and ask your vet for reminders.
